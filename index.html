About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at the University of Chicago. I study how authoritarian countries can strategically leverage institutional adjustments to mitigate risks and shape foreign economic relations.

Research

Working Papers

  • Quick Fix or Overhaul: Judicial Adjustments to Persuade Foreign Investors
    Analyzes how authoritarian regimes strategically use long-term reforms versus targeted interventions to shape investor beliefs about local business environment.
  • Where Corruption Feels Like Home: Institutional Similarities Driving Investment
    Explores how investors from China and the U.S. respond more to institutional familiarity than to institutional quality, using FCPA amendment-era FDI data.
  • Negotiating Across Dual Dimensions (with Shiyan Cao)
    A dynamic model of international cooperation where economic ties reshape bargaining space over trade and policy alignment between developing and advanced economies.

Publications

  • “Political Participation in China” (with Dali L. Yang). In Changes and Continuities of Social and Political Values in China, eds. Ronald Inglehart and Yang Zhong. University of Michigan Press, 2024.
  • “The Enigma of Political Trust in China: Response Patterns and Preferences for Redistribution” (with Dali L. Yang). Taiwan Journal of Democracy, December 2019.
