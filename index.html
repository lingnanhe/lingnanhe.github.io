About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at the University of Chicago. My research explores how authoritarian governments strategically adjust legal and institutional environments to attract foreign investment, focusing on judicial reforms, transnational law, and global trade politics.

Research

  • Persistent Overhaul or a Quick Fix: Explains how authoritarian regimes use systematic legal reform vs. one-off judicial interventions to influence foreign investor confidence.
  • Where Corruption Feels Like Home: Examines how institutional familiarity, not just quality, drives Chinese and American FDI patterns using historical FCPA shocks.
  • Exporting Disputes: Models when foreign firms rely on courts vs. informal protection in weak-rule-of-law regions, drawing on Chinese court-level appeal and win rate data.
